President

Glen Howard

i. Serve as the primary contact person for the organization and works with the COSO Liaison.

ii. Preside over the organization and Executive Board meetings.

iii. Ensure that a quorum is present before officially calling a meeting to order.

iv. Act as a liaison between the organization and the University.

v. Protect the welfare of the organization.

vi. Serve as chairperson(s) of the Executive Board.

vii. Represent the organization at functions at which such representation is needed.

viii. In the event of co-presidents, only one need to attend said events.

ix. Shall be an ex-officio member of all committees.

x. All powers not explicitly delegated to the other offices are delegated to the president(s). Co-Presidents will share equally in the responsibilities. Organize and implement the Mentor/Mentee Program for the year.

First Vice-President

Aaliyah Johnson

i. Assist the President, when appropriate, with the performance of their duties.

ii. In the absence of the President, will serve in their place.

iii. Assume the office of President when the president is incapacitated, absent or there is a vacancy in that office.

iv. Serve as chairperson for Homecoming and Martin Luther King Jr. Celebration.

Second Vice-President

Kara Roberts

i. Serve as programming chairperson of Black Awareness Month.

ii. He / She / They shall meet with their committee members and report back to the president.

iii. Can appoint a co-chair or vice chair with confirmation by a simply voting majority of the current executive board members.

Treasurer

Grace Ugwueke

i. Works with the SAFAC Liaison.

ii. Collect dues at the beginning of each semester.

iii. Keep record of the organization's finances.

iv. Make a financial report at each executive board meeting.

v. Administer the organization's funds upon approval of the president and advisor Complete all financial paper work (Berfs, IDR, Check Req's, and P.O's).

Secretary

Landon Coles

i. Serve as the recording officer of the organization and the custodian of its records, except those specifically assigned to others.

ii. Keep minutes and records of all proceedings of the organization.

iii. Maintain the organization’s official membership roll.

iv. Notify officers, committee members, and others of their elections or appointment; furnish committees with necessary documents pertinent to the performance of duties.

v. Call meetings to order in the absence of the President(s), First Vice-President and Second Vice-President and in such cases shall preside over the body’s proceedings until the body has selected a Chair Protem.

vi. Must make all reservations for organizational proceedings and special events.

vii. Shall be charged with the annual review and edit of the Constitution, if necessary, to be submitted to the Council of Student Organizations.

Public Relations Chair

Zoria Telfare

i. This position and all its duties shall be chaired by one member.

ii. Shall be responsible for promoting all United Black Students events including but not limited to meetings, programs, and parties using communication tools such as but not limited to flyers, newsletters, banners, social media and tabling events.

iii. Keep written, digital and photographic documentation of all events that take place within the organization.

iv. Make a yearbook and/or scrapbook accumulating all events.

v. Create a presentation board and presentation at any end of year awards banquet showcasing the organization.

vi. Update the United Black Students’ suite bulletin board showcasing Executive Board officers, recently past events and upcoming events.

G. Outreach/Project Coordinator Co-Chairs

i. Shall be responsible for co-sponsorship outreach and collaboration with out on-campus, student organizations.

ii. Shall serve as de-facto volunteers for all special programming events, MLK Week and Black Awareness Month programming.
