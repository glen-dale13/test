Our History

53 years ago, United Black Students first emerged at University of Miami as a support system and safe space for students of the African diaspora. In 1967, Harold Long and a small but strong group of students charted United Black Students, â€œthey wanted more black students to be enrolled, more scholarships for minorities, African American history courses, and black professors to teach them". Today, United Black Students (UBS) is now the third largest student organization on campus with 540 members and sits under the Office of Multicultural Student Affairs with Mr. Clarke serving as our advisor.

Our Mission

United Black Students aims to expose all members of the University of Miami community to the many facets of African American culture. Concurrently, we serve to unite, empower, and uplift African Americans through cultural, professional, and personal events and initiatives.
